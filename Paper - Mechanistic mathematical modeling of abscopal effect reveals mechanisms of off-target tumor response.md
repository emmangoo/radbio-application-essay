- The abscopal effect is rare, but has shown up in clinical settings
- First described in 1953
- It is modelled in this paper but still a rare phenomenon
- "Four mechanisms dominate outcome variability: antigen capture/processing by phagocytes (i.e., dendritic cells and macrophages), clearance of dead-cell debris and antigens, and naïve T-cell regenerative capacity in lymph nodes."
- General idea: local radiation can increase availability of antigens, which then circulate through the blood stream to target distant metastases/ secondary tumours
