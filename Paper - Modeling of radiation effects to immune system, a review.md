- lymphocytes specifically are sensitive to radiation- even small doses rapidly reduce blood count
- irradiating a certain area/ clump of cells such as a tumor also causes radiation effects on the surrounding blood vessels, and hence the cells within them. the amount of exact radiation received by surrounding cells is not exactly measurable.
- Radiation-induced lymphopenia can happen after radiotherapy, and is one of the measures used to quantify a patient's immune system
- specific therapies/ schemata such as FLASH-RT could reduce the death of circulating immune cells
