- "The immune system of the A-bomb survivors was damaged proportionately to the intensity of the A-bomb ionizing irradiation"
- Depending on the dose, the immune system can regenerate the hematopoietic cells, but stem cell populations bear radiation-induced DNA damage even several decades after exposure => as T and B cells originate from stem cells, their functions are impaired due to alterations in composition => decreased CD4 helper T cell population => this can lead to persistent inflammation and increased levels of inflammatory cytokines
- it is uncertain whether descendants of HSC have defects, but the HSC themselves clearly have defects such as chromosome aberrations and somatic mutations
- "Statistical analysis using gender- and age-adjusted values for individuals revealed a radiation-dose dependent decrease in the proportion of CD4 T cells"
- naive T cell populations decrease with age and radiation dose 
- B cell populations usually decrease with age, but "the proportion of B cells in the peripheral blood lymphocyte fraction increased as the intensity of radiation exposure increased"
- *not* every single aspect of the immune system is changed long-term by high doses of radiation
